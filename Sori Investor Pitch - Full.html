The problem

Sales professionals spend more time preparing than selling.

The average B2B sales professional spends 60–70% of their working time on non-selling tasks: researching prospects, updating CRMs, writing follow-ups, taking meeting notes, and trying to figure out who has budget. The tools that exist today give them data. Nobody gives them execution.

Research overload

Before every meeting, a salesperson needs to know who the prospect is, what they've done, and how to approach them. Today this is done manually: LinkedIn, Google, scattered notes. It takes hours per prospect.

CRM is a graveyard

CRMs are only as good as what gets entered. Sales professionals don't update them because it takes time they don't have. Pipelines go stale. Deals fall through the cracks.

The blank page problem

After every meeting, every lead response, every deal update — someone has to write something. A follow-up, a summary, a memo. Most salespeople stare at a blank page and start from scratch every time.

The solution

Sori doesn't give you better data.
It does the work.

Sori is an orchestration system of 10 specialized AI agents. The operator speaks a command. Sori dispatches the right agents, pulls the right data, generates the output, and delivers it via WhatsApp — waiting for human approval before anything leaves the system.

Profile Intelligence Data Stack & GDPR Decisions▼

Data sources evaluated

FullContact was ideal — 900+ attributes per person (interests, lifestyle, financial behavior). But it violates GDPR for EU prospects because personal data is aggregated without consent. Permanently dropped.

MVP1: Proxycurl only — LinkedIn profile scraping. Public professional data. Safe.

  • Current role + employment history
  • Education + declared skills
  • Public activity (posts, engagement)
  • Enables trajectory inference + seniority level

MVP2: Expanded stack

  • Cognism: Intent signals (hiring, funding rounds), verified European contact data
  • Clearbit: Company enrichment (tech stack, revenue range, size)
  • PDL: Deep career history, inferred skills, global coverage
  • Crunchbase + press releases: Public investment history
  • Calendar API: Behavioral patterns from operator's past meetings (first-party consented data — zero GDPR risk)

What stays out

  • Facebook/Instagram scraping — violates Meta ToS, requires legal review
  • Prospect location/behavioral data — GDPR restrictions
  • Bank data — requires PSD2 compliance + formal legal review

When public investment data doesn't exist, Financial Advisor infers budget from company size + seniority, with confidence level flagged.

5

Financial Intelligence NEW

Financial Advisor analyzes prospect budget capacity using CRM history, profile signals, and (optionally) operator's bank data. Outputs: budget estimate, risk rating, pricing recommendation. Click below for data details

Financial Advisor Data Inputs & PSD2 Status▼

Data inputs (MVP1)

  • CRM deal history: past deal sizes, close rates, payment history
  • Profile signals: company size, industry, seniority, years in role
  • Public data: funding rounds (from Crunchbase in MVP2), press releases

Outputs

  • Estimated prospect budget (with confidence level: inferred vs. verified)
  • Deal risk rating (probability of closure based on historical patterns)
  • Recommended pricing tier (based on capacity + risk)

Bank connections (blocked for MVP1+MVP2)

Operator bank data (via Plaid in US, Tink/Salt Edge in EU) would add cash flow context. BUT: PSD2 compliance in Europe requires formal legal review from a GDPR-specialist lawyer before any build decision. Blocked until legal sign-off obtained.

Same for prospect bank data — GDPR restrictions prevent us from analyzing third-party financial information without explicit legal framework.
